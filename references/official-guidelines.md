## TPAMI Scope Baseline

TPAMI publishes work in traditional areas of computer vision and image understanding, pattern analysis and recognition, and selected areas of machine intelligence, especially machine learning for pattern analysis.

Use this scope check:

- Is the central contribution methodological, analytical, or empirically generalizable?
- Is the application tied to a broader pattern-analysis, recognition, image-understanding, or machine-intelligence problem?
- Would the paper still matter beyond one dataset, product, or narrow implementation?

## Submission and Format Baseline

- IEEE Computer Society journals use page and reference limits that vary by journal; verify the current TPAMI author-information page before final submission.
- The Computer Society author page states regular Transactions overlength policy using a 12 formatted-page regular paper limit for final layout charges, but submission page limits may differ by journal.
- Journal abstracts should state the nature and significance of the paper. The Computer Society author page lists 100-200 words for regular/special-issue journal papers and 50 words for short papers.
- Double-column papers translate more readily into final publication format. Use official IEEE journal templates rather than manually altering format.
- High-resolution figures and readable text/symbols matter. The Computer Society author page gives 300 dpi at intended display size as a baseline.
- Appendixes in journal articles may be treated as supplemental material. Verify current policy before keeping appendix content in the main file.

## Ethics, Prior Versions, and Disclosure

- Concurrent or duplicate submission is a serious publication-ethics issue. Do not advise submitting the same work elsewhere while under IEEE review.
- If a manuscript expands a prior conference, workshop, arXiv, or other version, require citation/disclosure of the prior work and a clear explanation of substantive new contributions.
- Copyright permissions are the author's responsibility for reused text, figures, charts, images, and non-public-domain material.
- IEEE states that AI-generated article content, including text, figures, images, and code, must be disclosed in the acknowledgments section, identifying the system, affected sections, and level of use.
- IEEE generally treats AI use for editing and grammar enhancement differently; disclosure is commonly recommended but may not be required under the baseline policy. Verify before final submission.
- Do not treat this skill as legal advice. Flag risks and point to official IEEE policy.

## Practical Compliance Checklist

- Title: concise, specific, no overstated generality.
- Abstract: within current word limit, no unsupported superlatives, no references or equations unless official guidance allows.
- Keywords: aligned with IEEE/TPAMI taxonomy and reviewer discovery.
- Main text: claims supported by experiments, theory, or citations near the claim.
- Tables and figures: cited in text, readable in double-column format, captions explain protocol and takeaway.
- References: real, complete, IEEE-style, and relevant; no hallucinated citations.
- Supplement: separate nonessential material, appendixes, expanded proofs, extra figures, or videos when policy requires.
- Cover or submission notes: disclose prior versions, related submissions, conflicts, ethics approvals, permissions, and AI-generated content as applicable.
